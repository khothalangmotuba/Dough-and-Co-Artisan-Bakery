How We Make It Work

A sustainable bakery business that puts people and quality first.

Revenue Streams

Direct Sales

Single loaves sold at our bakery location in Leribe Hlotse. Our most accessible option for customers.

Contribution: 45%

Subscription Model

Weekly bread subscriptions with home delivery. Creates predictable recurring revenue and customer loyalty.

Contribution: 30%

Wholesale

Supplying local cafes, restaurants, and shops with fresh bread daily.

Contribution: 20%

Workshops

Monthly bread-making classes teaching customers how to bake at home. Builds community and brand loyalty.

Contribution: 5%

Where Our Money Goes

  • Ingredients (35%) - Quality flour and locally sourced ingredients. We never compromise on quality.
  • Labor (30%) - Fair wages for our small team. We believe bakers should be valued.
  • Rent & Utilities (15%) - Our kitchen space in Leribe Hlotse and equipment.
  • Packaging & Delivery (10%) - Eco-friendly packaging and delivery logistics.
  • Community & Marketing (5%) - Free bread donations, workshops, and local advertising.
  • Profit & Growth (5%) - Reinvested into the business for future expansion.

Target Market

Health-Conscious Consumers

Individuals seeking organic, preservative-free, and traditionally fermented foods.

Local Food Supporters

Customers who prioritize buying local and supporting small businesses in Leribe.

Food Service Businesses

Cafes and restaurants looking for high-quality, artisanal bread to serve their customers.

Home Bakers

Workshop attendees who want to learn and become brand advocates.

What Makes Us Different

✓ True Sourdough

24-48 hour fermentation, no commercial yeast. Most "sourdough" isn't real sourdough.

✓ Family Recipe

I use my grandmother's recipe that's been passed down for generations.

✓ Community First

5% of profits donated locally. We're building a business that gives back to Leribe.

✓ Hand-Shaped Only

No machines. Every loaf is shaped by hand with care and attention.

Future Plans

  • 2025: Open our first brick-and-mortar storefront in Leribe Hlotse
  • 2026: Expand wholesale to 15+ local businesses across Leribe
  • 2027: Launch a bread-making school for aspiring bakers in the community
  • 2028: Expand to a second location in another district
