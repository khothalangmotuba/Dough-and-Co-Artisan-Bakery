About Me & My Bakery

A story of passion, patience, and really good bread.

Why I Call It "Dough & Co."

The name means two things to me. First, obviously, there's the dough we bake. But "dough" is also slang for money, and "& Co." represents everyone who's helped me get here—my grandmother who is still alive and taught me everything, my family who believed in me, my first customers who took a chance on me, and all of you who keep showing up.

This business isn't just mine. It's ours.

The Full Story

I believe bread shouldn't have ingredients you can't pronounce. It shouldn't sit on a shelf for two weeks. It shouldn't be made by machines in factories far away.

Real bread is:

  • Simple: Flour, water, salt, time. That's it.
  • Honest: Made by human hands, not assembly lines
  • Local: Supporting farmers in our own community

I started Dough & Co. in 2024 because my grandmother taught me that real food brings people together. She's 78 years old now, and every time I bake, I think of her standing in her kitchen, hands covered in flour, telling me, "Good things take time, my child." I want to carry on her legacy and share her wisdom with my community here in Leribe Hlotse.

What I've Learned

Consistency Over Perfection

Success doesn't come from one perfect loaf. It comes from showing up every day and making good bread consistently.

People Can Taste the Care

When something is made with genuine passion, people feel it. That's what keeps them coming back.

Support Goes Both Ways

My community has supported me from day one. Now it's my turn to give back.

Love What You Do

Waking up early gets easier when you actually love what you're waking up for.
